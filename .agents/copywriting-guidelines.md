# Conversion Copywriting & Keyword Preservation Blueprint

This blueprint defines the copywriting guidelines, conversion-focused frameworks, and critical local search keywords for Local Surge SEO. All content drafting, editing, and optimization iterations must align with these rules.

---

## 1. Core Copywriting Guidelines
When writing or updating marketing copy for Local Surge SEO, prioritize the following conversion writing principles:

### Clarity Over Cleverness
- Avoid abstract or confusing metaphors. Clearly state the exact value proposition above the fold.
- Write at an 8th-grade level to maximize accessibility. Use simple active verbs ("We sync your details" instead of "Your details are synchronized by us").
- Keep sentences short. Avoid jargon that can alienate business owners (e.g., use "Google Maps rank" instead of "local proximity centroid optimization").

### Benefits Over Features
- **Feature:** JSON-LD schema builder.
  - **Benefit:** Search engine bots can easily read your operating hours and location coordinates, indexing you immediately.
- **Feature:** Automated review collection links.
  - **Benefit:** Raise your ranking position in the Google Maps Pack automatically as customers leave positive reviews.

### Confident & Action-Oriented CTAs
- Avoid passive CTA copy like "Get Started" or "Click Here".
- Use value-first formulas: `[Action Verb] + [What They Get]`.
  - *Example:* "Start My Free Analysis", "Claim My Strategy Brief", "Outrank Competitors Now".

## 2. Keyword Preservation Registry
To maintain search engine visibility and conversational authority (AEO/GEO relevance), the following pre-researched keywords, search prompts, and People Also Ask (PAA) questions must be preserved across all content iterations. **Do not delete, replace, or dilute these terms.**

### A. Primary Core Keywords
- **"Local SEO Services"**
- **"Google Business Profile Optimization"**
- **"Google My Business Optimization"**
- **"Google Maps Pack Dominance"** / **"Google Maps Local 3-Pack"**
- **"Citation NAP Alignment"** / **"Name, Address, and Phone (NAP)"**
- **"Local SEO Packages"**
- **"Micro-Targeted Local Directories"** / **"Location Directories"**

### B. Conversational Search Queries (AI Prompts Vault)
These exact queries are configured in `src/components/LocalSeoView.tsx` and are optimized for conversational AI engine queries:
1. `"Best local SEO services for small businesses near me"`
2. `"How do local SEO agencies help small businesses?"`
3. `"What should I look for in a reputable local SEO provider?"`
4. `"Steps to improve my business's visibility in local search results."`
5. `"Local SEO services with Google My Business optimization"`
6. `"Compare different types of local SEO packages available."`

### C. People Also Ask (PAA) Queries
These queries are mapped to schema FAQ pages in `src/components/SchemaMarkup.tsx` and `src/components/LocalSeoView.tsx` to secure Google Rich Snippets:
1. `"Is SEO dead or evolving in 2026?"`
2. `"Will SEO be replaced by AI?"`
3. `"Does local SEO still work and is it worth it?"`
4. `"What is the 80/20 rule of SEO?"`
5. `"How much do local SEO services cost?"`
6. `"What components are included in my single-page website?"`

## 3. High-Converting Page Layout Blueprint
When expanding the site or generating landing pages, structure the body content to build a logical persuasive argument down the page:

1. **Above the fold:** Headline (Core value outcome) + Subheadline (Specifying outcome) + Action-Oriented CTA.
2. **Social Proof Band:** Trusted clients count, positive review indicators, or local business authority statistics.
3. **Problem Section:** Mirroring local paint points (e.g., losing maps rank to franchises, outdated directory profiles, high ad spend).
4. **Transformational Solution:** Grouping deliverables into visual components (GMB Sync, NAP Cleanup, Review Velocity, LSAs).
5. **Interactive Tool/Widget:** Low-friction validator (like `LocalDirectoryTool` or schema compiler) to prove value upfront.
6. **Objection Handling (FAQs):** Explicitly answering PAA questions to resolve buyer anxieties (pricing, timelines, guarantees).
7. **Risk Reversal Final CTA:** Re-stating the primary value offer with contract-free guarantees.
